From the Editor

For thirty years I made my living looking carefully at small things — corneas, retinas, the thin film that separates seeing from not-seeing. I am beginning to think the habit of looking carefully is the only thing worth taking into retirement.

These dispatches are short. A photograph and a paragraph. A note from a ride. A case I cannot stop thinking about. A house I am leaving, and a place I have not yet found.

— T.J.O'B.

The Last Cataract

There will be a final surgery — a final eye, a final lens — and I will not know it is the last one until afterward.

The schedule for January 2027 is already taking shape on the screen in the front office. Somewhere in that grid of fifteen-minute blocks is a name I have not met yet, attached to an eye I have not yet examined. That patient will be my last.

I have done somewhere north of fifteen thousand cataract surgeries. I can no longer remember the first one with any clarity — only that I was terrified, and that the resident behind me was kinder than I deserved...
